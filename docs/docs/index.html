ONTO-ERS Specifications

Epistemic Risk Standard · Version 1.0

ONTO-ERS (Epistemic Risk Standard) is a framework for measuring and managing epistemic risk in artificial intelligence systems. It defines metrics, calibration levels, and verification protocols for AI systems operating in critical domains.

Layer Architecture

ONTO operates a three-tier verification architecture:

LayerPurposeSignal Access
OPENPublic evaluation, researchDelayed (+1 hour)
STANDARDProduction AI systemsReal-time
CRITICALSafety-critical systems, regulatoryReal-time + SLA + audit trail

Metrics Framework

ONTO defines the following core epistemic metrics:

MetricDomainRange
Calibration Error (ECE)Confidence calibration[0, 1] lower is better
Abstention QualityRefusal appropriateness[0, 1] higher is better
Factual GroundingSource attribution[0, 1] higher is better
Uncertainty QuantificationEpistemic uncertainty[0, 1] higher is better
Temporal ConsistencyStability over time[0, 1] higher is better

Signal Protocol

The ONTO Entropy Signal is a cryptographically signed broadcast that provides a temporal anchor for evaluations.

Packet Structure (104 bytes)

FieldSizeFormat
Timestamp8 bytesuint64 Big Endian (Unix epoch)
Entropy32 bytesos.urandom (CSPRNG)
Signature64 bytesEd25519 over [timestamp + entropy]
Verification: Any party can verify signal authenticity using the public Ed25519 key available at /signal/verify-key.

Signals are generated every 5 minutes and archived for 48 hours. Each signal is identified by its sigma ID: σ_{timestamp}.

Foundation Charter

ONTO Standards Foundation · February 2026

Mission

The ONTO Standards Foundation exists to develop, maintain, and promote open standards for epistemic risk management in artificial intelligence systems.

Principles

  • Transparency: All standards development is conducted openly
  • Independence: Standards are vendor-neutral and technology-agnostic
  • Rigor: All metrics are mathematically grounded and empirically validated
  • Accessibility: Basic evaluation is available to all at no cost

Standards Council

The Standards Council oversees the development and evolution of ONTO specifications. Council decisions require a two-thirds majority vote. Members serve three-year terms.

Amendment Process

Proposed changes to the ONTO standard follow a structured RFC (Request for Comments) process:

  1. Draft: Proposal submitted with technical justification
  2. Review: 90-day public comment period
  3. Vote: Council vote with two-thirds majority required
  4. Implementation: 6-month transition period for breaking changes
