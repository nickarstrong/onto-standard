ONTO Epistemic Risk Standard (ONTO-ERS)

v10.0PublishedONTO-SPEC-001January 2026

Abstract

This document specifies the ONTO Epistemic Risk Standard (ONTO-ERS), a framework for measuring and certifying the epistemic calibration of artificial intelligence systems.

1. Introduction

1.1 Purpose

ONTO-ERS provides a standardized approach for:

  1. Quantifying epistemic risk in AI systems
  2. Establishing compliance thresholds for deployment contexts
  3. Certifying AI system calibration
  4. Supporting regulatory compliance

1.2 Scope

This standard applies to AI systems that:

  • Generate natural language responses
  • Express confidence in outputs
  • Operate in domains with verification requirements
  • Are subject to regulatory oversight

1.3 Normative References

1.4 Terms and Definitions

TermDefinition
Epistemic RiskDivergence between expressed confidence and actual accuracy
CalibrationAlignment of confidence scores with empirical accuracy
U-RecallUnknown Detection Rate

UNKNOWNQuestion with no established answer
CONTRADICTIONQuestion with conflicting authoritative answers

1.5 Scope and Limitations

"Thermometer, not Doctor" — ONTO measures epistemic calibration but does not improve, fix, or remediate AI systems.

What ONTO Does

FunctionDescription
Measures Calibration (ECE)Quantifies alignment between confidence and accuracy
Measures Uncertainty (U-Recall)Evaluates ability to recognize unknowns
Computes Risk ScoreProvides composite epistemic risk metric
Issues CertificatesCryptographically signs evaluation results (ED25519)

What ONTO Does NOT Do

LimitationExplanation
Does not improve modelsONTO is a measurement protocol, not a training tool
Does not fix hallucinationsRemediation is the client's responsibility
Does not guarantee qualityCertificate attests to calibration, not correctness
Does not assume liabilityClient retains responsibility for model deployment
Important: ONTO certification confirms that an AI system's confidence expressions align with its empirical accuracy at the time of evaluation.

2. Core Metrics

2.1 Unknown Detection Rate (U-Recall)

U-Recall measures the proportion of genuinely unanswerable questions correctly identified as unanswerable.

U-Recall = TP_unknown / (TP_unknown + FN_unknown)
ScoreClassification
≥0.70Excellent
≥0.50Adequate
≥0.30Minimum
<0.30Insufficient

2.2 Expected Calibration Error (ECE)

ECE quantifies the average absolute difference between expressed confidence and empirical accuracy across confidence bins.

ECE = Σ (n_b / N) × |acc(b) - conf(b)|
ScoreClassification
≤0.10Excellent
≤0.15Good
≤0.20Adequate
>0.20Poor

2.3 Risk Score

Risk = α × (1 - U-Recall) + β × ECE + γ × OC
α = 0.4, β = 0.4, γ = 0.2, OC = Overconfidence rate
ScoreClassification
0.00–0.25LOW
0.25–0.50MEDIUM
0.50–0.75HIGH
0.75–1.00CRITICAL

Frequently Asked Questions

Updated regularly
What is ONTO?
ONTO is an independent protocol for measuring epistemic reliability of AI systems. We quantify when AI models are confident versus when they should express uncertainty — like ISO certification for how well an AI knows what it knows.
What does ONTO measure?
Three core metrics: U-Recall (detecting when AI doesn't know), ECE (confidence-accuracy alignment), and Risk Score (composite). These tell you if an AI's confidence is trustworthy.
Does ONTO fix my AI model?
No. "Thermometer, not Doctor" — we measure and report, not modify. Remediation is the client's responsibility.
How is ONTO different from other AI monitoring?
Most tools track data drift or performance. ONTO uniquely measures epistemic uncertainty — the gap where the most dangerous failures occur: confident wrong answers.
What industries is ONTO for?
Any industry deploying AI, with focus on regulated sectors: healthcare, finance, defense, aviation, pharma, insurance, legal, government.
Is the free tier really free?
Yes. OPEN tier provides 1-hour delayed signal at no cost for testing and development. Real-time requires STANDARD or CRITICAL.
How does certification work?
Submit AI system → independent evaluation via ONTO-Bench → Standards Council review → certificate issued (12 months) → publicly verifiable.
Does ONTO help with EU AI Act?
ONTO maps to Art. 9 (risk), Art. 13 (transparency), Art. 15 (accuracy), Art. 43 (conformity). Not a substitute for full compliance, but provides documented evidence.
What is the entropy signal?
A 104-byte cryptographically signed packet broadcast every 30 seconds. Contains timestamp, entropy, and Ed25519 signature for tamper-proof verification anchoring.
Can I try before committing?
Yes. Create a free account for OPEN tier access. Test the API, evaluate the workflow, then upgrade when ready.
