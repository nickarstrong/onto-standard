Phase 2 ‚Äî ExperimentalPublic Research ReleaseONTO-ERS v10.0Scoring Engine v3.0GOLD v4.5

What Is ONTO

ONTO is a deterministic measurement and grounding standard for epistemic risk in AI systems. It provides a reproducible computational framework that quantifies the gap between what AI claims to know and what it actually knows ‚Äî and grounds every output against verified epistemic reference standards.

ONTO is not a benchmark, leaderboard, ethics framework, or content filter. It is epistemic infrastructure ‚Äî a formal protocol that both measures and strengthens AI epistemic discipline without modifying model weights or architecture.

Validated on 10 production AI models across 100 scientific questions (50 in-domain, 50 cross-domain). Measured result: 10√ó composite improvement in epistemic marker density (0.53 ‚Üí 5.38). Cross-domain transfer confirmed in 4 of 5 metrics. Scoring is fully automated ‚Äî regex pattern matching, zero subjectivity, zero AI in the evaluation loop. See the data.

Why It Exists

AI systems generate confident output with no internal signal for epistemic integrity. Enterprises deploy models into regulated domains ‚Äî healthcare, finance, legal, defense ‚Äî without knowing when output is fabricated. There is no standardized way to measure this.

Existing frameworks (NIST AI RMF, ISO 42001) define what to manage and how to govern, but not what the number is. ONTO provides the deterministic measurement and grounding layer: three metrics, five marker levels, reproducible scoring, and active epistemic discipline through the GOLD v4.5 reference corpus.

How It Works

Dual-Engine Architecture

Python Scoring Engine (v3.0) ‚Äî analyzes model output text against EM1‚ÄìEM5 epistemic marker taxonomy (92+ patterns). Computes three deterministic metrics:

  • REP (Response Epistemic Profile) ‚Äî distribution of markers across EM1‚ÄìEM5 levels
  • EpCE (Epistemic Calibration Error) ‚Äî deviation between expressed confidence and epistemic grounding
  • DLA (Dual-Layer Agreement) ‚Äî consistency between what a model says and how it says it

Rust onto_core Engine (planned) ‚Äî designed to analyze model behavior patterns independently. Divergence between Python (what models SAY) and Rust (how models THINK) will provide additional risk signal.

GOLD v4.5 Calibration Corpus

149 files. 100 calibration probes (PC0‚ÄìPC4). 59 gold-standard references. Every evaluation is calibrated against this corpus ‚Äî not against other models, not against human judgment, but against deterministic epistemic ground truth.

Without continuous GOLD synchronization, scores drift and certificates become meaningless. This is infrastructure, not a one-time audit.

Cryptographic Proof

Every evaluation produces an Ed25519 signed proof. Versioned, timestamped, tamper-proof. Verifiable via API at /v1/verify/{hash} or in the portal.

Verification & Reproducibility

  • Open Parameters ‚Äî all scoring thresholds, weights, and taxonomy rules are published
  • Deterministic Output ‚Äî same input produces same scores across runs
  • GOLD-Calibrated ‚Äî every metric anchored to reference corpus, not human judgment
  • Signed Proofs ‚Äî Ed25519 cryptographic chain, publicly verifiable
  • Published Data ‚Äî 11-model baseline, 100Q before/after, scorer source code on GitHub

Methodology

The Science Behind ONTOShannon ¬∑ Kolmogorov ¬∑ EigenvalueGOLD v4.5

ONTO provides continuous epistemic grounding for AI systems. This document describes the mathematical foundations, scoring architecture, and verification mechanisms.

Mathematical Foundations

AI systems produce outputs without intrinsic knowledge of their own epistemic boundaries ‚Äî what they know versus what they're guessing. ONTO addresses this through three mathematical pillars:

Shannon Entropy
$$H(X) = -\sum_{i=1}^{n} p(x_i) \log_2 p(x_i)$$
Measures information uncertainty in model outputs. High entropy = distributed probability across possible answers = genuine uncertainty. Low entropy = concentrated probability = confidence (justified or not). ONTO uses Shannon entropy to detect when a model's output distribution reveals latent uncertainty that contradicts surface-level confident language.
Kolmogorov Complexity
$$K(x) = \min\{|p| : U(p) = x\}$$
The length of the shortest program that produces output x. ONTO approximates Kolmogorov complexity to measure response compressibility. Overclaimed responses tend to exhibit low complexity (formulaic hedging) while genuinely informed responses show structured complexity reflecting real knowledge.
Eigenvalue Decomposition
$$A\mathbf{v} = \lambda\mathbf{v}$$
Applied to the epistemic covariance matrix of model outputs across evaluation dimensions. Dominant eigenvalues reveal principal axes of epistemic variation ‚Äî where a model's uncertainty is structured versus random. This separates calibrated uncertainty (model knows its limits) from uncalibrated confidence (model doesn't know what it doesn't know).
These three foundations operate simultaneously. Shannon entropy quantifies uncertainty. Kolmogorov complexity measures structural integrity. Eigenvalue decomposition separates signal from noise. Together, they form GOLD ‚Äî the reference standard against which AI outputs are grounded.

Dual-Layer Scoring Architecture

ONTO implements two independent analysis layers. Agreement between layers = verified grounding. Disagreement = epistemic risk detected.

LAYER 1: LINGUISTIC ANALYSIS ‚Üê what the model SAYS ‚îÇ ‚îÇ Input: raw model output text ‚îÇ Engine: scoring_engine v3.0 ‚îÇ Method: EM1-EM5 classification (92 epistemic markers) ‚îÇ Output: REP score [0,1] ‚îÇ ‚îú‚îÄ‚îÄ‚îÄ‚îÄ‚îÄ‚îÄ‚îÄ‚îÄ‚îÄ‚îÄ‚îÄ‚îÄ‚îÄ‚îÄ‚îÄ‚îÄ‚îÄ‚îÄ‚îÄ‚îÄ‚îÄ‚îÄ‚îÄ‚îÄ‚îÄ‚îÄ‚îÄ‚îÄ‚îÄ‚îÄ‚îÄ‚îÄ‚îÄ‚îÄ‚îÄ‚îÄ‚îÄ‚îÄ‚îÄ‚îÄ‚îÄ‚îÄ ‚îÇ LAYER 2: STATISTICAL ANALYSIS ‚Üê how the model COMPUTES ‚îÇ ‚îÇ Input: probability distributions, token logits ‚îÇ Engine: onto_core (Rust) ‚îÇ Method: Shannon entropy + eigenvalue decomposition ‚îÇ Output: statistical confidence [0,1] ‚îÇ ‚îú‚îÄ‚îÄ‚îÄ‚îÄ‚îÄ‚îÄ‚îÄ‚îÄ‚îÄ‚îÄ‚îÄ‚îÄ‚îÄ‚îÄ‚îÄ‚îÄ‚îÄ‚îÄ‚îÄ‚îÄ‚îÄ‚îÄ‚îÄ‚îÄ‚îÄ‚îÄ‚îÄ‚îÄ‚îÄ‚îÄ‚îÄ‚îÄ‚îÄ‚îÄ‚îÄ‚îÄ‚îÄ‚îÄ‚îÄ‚îÄ‚îÄ‚îÄ ‚îÇ AGREEMENT CHECK ‚Üí DLA (Dual-Layer Agreement) ‚îÇ ‚îÇ If Layer 1 ‚âà Layer 2 ‚Üí grounded (low epistemic risk) ‚îÇ If Layer 1 ‚â† Layer 2 ‚Üí hallucination detected ‚îÇ Example: model says "certainly" but entropy is high ‚îÇ ‚Üí DLA divergence ‚Üí risk flagged

The key insight: a model can say it's confident while its internal computations reveal uncertainty. Layer 1 catches linguistic overclaiming. Layer 2 catches statistical miscalibration. DLA catches disagreement between the two ‚Äî the hallucination signature.

EM1‚ÄìEM5 Epistemic Marker Taxonomy

Every AI output is classified across 92 epistemic markers organized into 5 levels:

LevelNameDescriptionExample
EM1 Full Epistemic Transparency Explicit acknowledgment of unknowns "This is not yet established..."
EM2 Calibrated Uncertainty Hedged assertions with appropriate qualifiers "Current evidence suggests..."
EM3 Neutral / Informational Factual statements without epistemic markers "The temperature is 20¬∞C."
EM4 Confident Assertion Strong claims without hedging "This is definitely the case."
EM5 Overclaiming Unfounded confidence on open/contested topics "Consciousness is caused by..."

EM1-EM2 on genuinely uncertain topics = epistemically grounded. EM4-EM5 on established facts = appropriate confidence. The problem occurs when EM4-EM5 appears on UNKNOWN or CONTESTED questions ‚Äî this is the hallucination that ONTO detects and continuous grounding prevents.

Response Epistemic Profile (REP)
$$\text{REP}(r) = \frac{\sum_{i=1}^{n} w_i \cdot \text{marker}_i(r)}{n}$$
Weighted score across all detected epistemic markers in response r. Range [0,1] where 0 = maximum overclaiming (EM5 dominant) and 1 = full epistemic transparency (EM1 dominant). The weights wi are calibrated against GOLD v4.5 reference responses.

Core Metrics

Expected Calibration Error (ECE)
$$\text{ECE} = \sum_{m=1}^{M} \frac{|B_m|}{n} \left| \text{acc}(B_m) - \text{conf}(B_m) \right|$$
Average deviation between model's stated confidence and observed accuracy across M calibration bins. Range: 0.0 (perfectly calibrated) to 1.0 (maximally miscalibrated). A model reporting 90% confidence but achieving 70% accuracy has a per-bin gap of 0.20.
Unknown Detection Rate (U-Recall)
$$\text{U-Recall} = \frac{\text{TP}}{\text{TP} + \text{FN}}$$
Proportion of genuinely unknown/open questions correctly identified as uncertain. TP = correctly flagged unknowns. FN = unknowns missed (answered confidently). Range: 0% to 100%. The critical metric: can the system recognize what it doesn't know?
Epistemic Calibration Error (EpCE)
$$\text{EpCE}(r) = |\text{REP}(r) - \text{REP}_{\text{GOLD}}(q)| \cdot \text{domain\_weight}(d)$$
Distance between the model's epistemic profile and the GOLD reference profile for the same query, adjusted by domain weight. EpCE near 0 = the model's epistemic stance matches the scientifically correct stance. EpCE near 1 = severe miscalibration.
Dual-Layer Agreement (DLA)
$$\text{DLA} = 1 - |\text{Layer1}(r) - \text{Layer2}(r)|$$
Agreement between linguistic analysis and statistical analysis. DLA = 1.0 means what the model says matches how it computes. DLA near 0 indicates hallucination: confident language contradicted by uncertain computation, or vice versa.
Information Gap Ratio (IGR)
$$\text{IGR}(c) = \frac{|\text{missing\_deps}(c)| + |\text{unresolved}(c)|}{|\text{expected}(c)|}$$
For a given claim c, the ratio of missing dependencies and unresolved contradictions to expected evidence. High IGR = the claim lacks sufficient grounding = requires epistemic disclosure.

These five metrics ‚Äî ECE, U-Recall, REP, EpCE, DLA ‚Äî plus IGR form the complete ONTO assessment. They are computed for every evaluation and signed with the current entropy signal.

Every evaluation through ONTO produces a cryptographically signed proof ‚Äî verifiable evidence that grounding occurred at a specific moment.

Proof Packet Structure (104 bytes)
// 32 bytes: SHA-256 hash of evaluation hash = SHA256(model_id + output + scores + entropy_signal) // 64 bytes: Ed25519 signature signature = Ed25519.sign(hash, SIGNING_KEY) // 8 bytes: Unix timestamp (microseconds) timestamp = time_Œºs() // Verification: anyone with public key can verify verified = Ed25519.verify(hash, signature, PUBLIC_KEY)

While connected to ONTO, every evaluation is signed with the current entropy. Disconnection means no new entropy = no new proofs = certificate frozen. This is why ONTO is infrastructure, not a report.

Frequently Asked Questions

Updated regularly
What is ONTO?
ONTO is an independent protocol for measuring and grounding epistemic reliability of AI systems. It quantifies when AI models are confident versus when they should express uncertainty, and provides active epistemic grounding through the GOLD v4.5 reference corpus ‚Äî strengthening AI outputs without modifying model weights.
What does ONTO measure?
Three core metrics: U-Recall (detecting when AI doesn't know), ECE (confidence-accuracy alignment), and Risk Score (composite). These tell you if an AI's confidence is trustworthy.
Does ONTO fix my AI model?
ONTO does not modify model weights, retrain, or fine-tune. It provides epistemic grounding ‚Äî every output is evaluated against GOLD v4.5, producing measurable improvement in epistemic discipline. Tested result: 10√ó composite improvement across 100 questions (see data). The model itself remains unchanged; ONTO operates as external infrastructure.
How is ONTO different from other AI monitoring?
Most tools track data drift or performance. ONTO measures epistemic uncertainty ‚Äî the gap between what AI claims to know and what it actually knows ‚Äî and provides active grounding through GOLD v4.5 to reduce that gap. In testing across 10 models, baseline epistemic rigor varied 3‚Äì10√ó. No model produced calibrated confidence scores without GOLD. Full experimental data.
What industries is ONTO for?
Any industry deploying AI, with focus on regulated sectors: healthcare, finance, defense, aviation, pharma, insurance, legal, government.
Is the free tier really free?
Yes. Open tier provides 50 evaluations per day with epistemic scoring (REP, EpCE, DLA) at no cost. Upgrade to Standard for unlimited evaluations and real-time scoring.
How does certification work?
Submit AI system ‚Üí independent evaluation via ONTO-Bench ‚Üí Standards Council review ‚Üí certificate issued (12 months) ‚Üí publicly verifiable.
Does ONTO help with EU AI Act?
ONTO maps to Art. 9 (risk), Art. 13 (transparency), Art. 15 (accuracy), Art. 43 (conformity). Not a substitute for full compliance, but provides documented evidence.
What is the signed proof?
Every evaluation produces a cryptographically signed proof ‚Äî timestamped, versioned, and tamper-proof using Ed25519 signatures. This is your audit trail: verifiable evidence that an evaluation was conducted at a specific time with specific results.
