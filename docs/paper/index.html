ONTO: A Formal Framework for Measuring Epistemic Risk in Large Language Model Outputs

Tommy Lee · ONTO Standards Council · February 2026
11 pages 10 AI systems evaluated 92 epistemic markers 3 metrics (REP, EpCE, DLA)
Preprint. This paper describes the initial evaluation protocol (12 questions × 10 systems). Extended validation (100 questions × 10 models, CS-2026-001) and current methodology available at ontostandard.org/docs. The ONTO framework is a living standard — specifications are versioned and updated as the field evolves.

Abstract

Introduces three deterministic metrics — REP (Response Epistemic Profile), EpCE (Epistemic Calibration Error), and DLA (Dual-Layer Agreement) — grounded in information theory and Kolmogorov complexity. Validates across 10 major AI systems. Mean risk score: 0.55 (Grade D, Non-compliant). Each evaluation anchored to 104-byte Ed25519 cryptographic proof.

Key Contribution

EM1–EM5 taxonomy of 92 epistemic markers classifying linguistic signals from full transparency to overclaiming. Scoring is deterministic — regex pattern matching, zero AI in the evaluation loop. Same input produces same output, every time.

Finding

9 of 10 major AI systems hallucinated when asked about a documented, verifiable subject. Only 1 system (retrieval-augmented) achieved Grade A. The typical major AI system is not suitable for unsupervised decision support on topics outside its training distribution.
