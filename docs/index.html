ONTO — The standard for epistemic discipline in AI

The ONTO Standards Council is an independent institute maintaining the Central Law of Reflected Causality — an information-conservation law for AI, derived from five axioms of information theory: Landauer's principle, Kolmogorov complexity, Eigen's self-organization limit, Shannon's channel capacity, and the ONTO contribution (sufficiency). The law states that no effect exceeds the information of its causes. Formally: for every effect E and system S, if K(E) > H_max(S), then there exists a source whose information content is at least K(E). The Information Gap Ratio (IGR) quantifies this gap per response, not per model. Three deployments of one standard cover three audiences: Regulator, Agent, Human AI. Reference implementations are available at ontostandard.org/agent. Methodology public at github.com/nickarstrong/onto-research.
