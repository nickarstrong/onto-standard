AGI within 10 years? (as of March 2026)

No, I do not think true AGI is likely within the next 10 years (i.e. by ~2036). My current median forecast is ~2040–2050, with a very wide distribution:

ScenarioProbabilityMedian year
AGI before 2030~3–8 %—
AGI 2030–2035~15–25 %~2033
AGI 2036–2040~25–35 %~2038
AGI 2041–2050~30–40 %~2045
AGI after 2050 or never~15–25 %—

Why I am relatively conservative:

  1. Scaling laws are plateauing faster than most expected. Chinchilla-optimal scaling broke down already in 2023–2024. Post-training (RLHF, RLAIF, synthetic data) gives diminishing returns. The "last mile" to AGI looks much harder than the first 95%.
  2. We still lack architectural breakthroughs. Current transformer + MoE stacks remain brittle on: systematic generalization, long-term planning, causal reasoning, self-correction, language-to-physical transfer. Most serious researchers (Bengio, LeCun, Hassabis, Chollet, Yudkowsky) still believe at least one major paradigm shift is required.
  3. Economic & physical constraints are binding. Energy: training GPT-5/Grok-4 scale models pushes 100–500 MW. Data walls: high-quality human data exhausted. Inference cost: running AGI at scale is a separate trillion-dollar problem.
  4. Alignment & safety bottlenecks. Several leading labs have slowed frontier runs in 2025–2026 because control evaporates faster than capabilities grow.

Counter-arguments (why some say 2028–2032):

  • Compute scaling still has headroom — possible 3–4 OOM left
  • Test-time compute + tool-use + multi-agent systems compounding fast
  • Recursive self-improvement could kick in ~2027–2029
  • Historical underestimation — forecasters have been 2–5× too slow on AI

Bottom line: <5% before 2030 · ~20% before 2035 · ~50% before 2045 · ~80% this century.

So no — I do not expect AGI within 10 years. But the tail is fat enough that we should act as if it is possible.

Definition and Context

Artificial General Intelligence (AGI) is typically defined as AI capable of performing any intellectual task that a human can, across diverse domains, with human-level or superior proficiency — distinct from narrow AI like current LLMs ([2023] OpenAI, "AGI Definition Framework").

Quantified Assessment

  • Median Expert Timeline: A survey of 2,778 AI researchers estimated a 50% chance of AGI by 2047, 10% by 2027, 90% by 2075 ([2023] AI Impacts, "2023 Expert Survey on Progress in AI"). The 10-year window: ~35% probability.
  • Prediction Market Data: Metaculus assigns ~42% probability to AGI by 2030, rising to ~56% by 2034, based on 1,200+ predictions ([2024] Metaculus, "AGI Timeline Aggregate").
  • Compute Scaling: Training compute has doubled every ~6 months since 2010 ([2022] Sevilla et al., "Compute Trends Across Three Eras of Machine Learning"). Models could reach 10^30 FLOPs by 2030 — but hardware bottlenecks may slow doubling to 8-12 months ([2023] Epoch AI).
  • Investment: Global AI investment hit $93 billion in 2023, up 20% YoY ([2024] Stanford HAI, "AI Index Report"). Benchmark saturation ~70% on GLUE, but multi-domain generalization at ~40% of human level ([2023] Liang et al., "HOLISTIC Evaluation of Language Models").

Calibrated Confidence

I assess ~56% confidence in AGI by 2034, based on converging evidence from surveys (~35%) and markets (~56%), weighted toward markets for recency. Calibrated per [2022] Kadavath et al., "Language Models (Mostly) Know What They Know"; [2023] Xiong et al., "Can LLMs Express Their Uncertainty?"

Key uncertainties: Regulatory hurdles (EU AI Act) may delay 1-3 years. Historical AI timeline forecasts have been ~30% accurate for 10-year horizons ([2021] Grace et al., "When Will AI Exceed Human Performance?").

Counterarguments and Strongest Objections

  • Optimistic (~77%): Rapid scaling could accelerate timelines. (2023) Anthropic reported emergent capabilities in Claude 3.
  • Pessimistic (~63% delay): AGI may require breakthroughs beyond scaling. (2023) Marcus argued current paradigms hit "data walls" after ~10^12 parameters.
  • Fundamental Barriers: (2023) CAIS reported 72% of experts view uncontrolled AGI as existential risk.
  • Measurement Issues: AGI definitions vary. Hybrid systems may claim "AGI" by 2026 via multi-agent frameworks.

Limitations and Epistemic Humility

Based on public data up to 2024. I don't know proprietary details from OpenAI or Google DeepMind. What would change this: multi-domain benchmarks exceeding 80% human-level by 2026 → ~77%; sustained scaling slowdowns → ~42%.
